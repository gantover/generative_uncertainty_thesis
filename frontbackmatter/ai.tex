\chapter*{Note on AI Use}

In this note, I am both making a review of how the AI usage went and highlighting how I used it throughout this thesis. 

My experience using AI was mixed, on some fronts like writing code it was really useful. Prototyping was very fast, and if the task was not too complicated, worked out of the box with a code or modification to the code that was easy to understand and verify. 

``Brainstorming'' with AI was also very useful, it allowed for quick prototyping mathematical proofs of instance. Although the proofs were usually wrong, they mostly went in the right direction.

Summarizing and suggesting papers to investigate was not good, the AI kept inventing facts and sometimes saying the exact opposite of what was written in a paper even when the fact was in plain english in a PDF that the AI had access to.

Writing with AI was not good either. It was almost impossible for the AI to understand the full context of both my thesis and surrounding papers.

The models used were Gemini Pro 3.1, Claude Sonet 4.6 and the AUTO model selection from Github Copilot. 

Gemini Pro 3.1 was the main model used for everything beside coding.

My usage of AI was very basic, using smarter ways to feed context to the LLM and not just giving it PDFs would most likely result in a much better experience. 

In summary, I could only get the tool to work properly on specific tasks that involved reasonably sized context.
